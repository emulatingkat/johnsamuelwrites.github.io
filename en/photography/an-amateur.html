An Amateur Photographer

John Samuel

I started photography some years back. The prime inspiration came from my father's analog photography, though most of his photographs revolved around family and friends. But he had stopped photography a long back. And all I know is that his camera no longer worked. Though this apparently inutile device left a lasting mark in my mind. And as soon as I could afford a mobile phone with a camera, I purchased one with quite humble camera features. Here again, I followed my father's footsteps. Most of my initial photographs revolved around my friends and family. Slowly I started travelling, making smaller trips to nearby towns and cities. I required a better apparatus to record my travelogues. Again, I purchased a smartphone with much better camera settings. Slowly, my pictures concentrated more on the objects around me than on myself: the trees, the roads, the monuments.

Then came the big change in my life: from traveling to cities to traveling to different countries. I required a better camera and I finally bought a better camera, this time an appartus targeted just for one and only thing: photography. Digital camera became part of my life. I could zoom without having a loss in the quality of my photographs. I started with automatic mode and practised with it for a very long time. Slowly I started reading blogs on photography and checking the works of other photographs. I learned about ISO settings and adjusting the focal length. It took me a significantly long time to be at ease with these settings. My initial photographs were based on my first impressions on arriving at a spot. I took a lot of photographs. Slowly I reduced the number of photographs by first looking around the spot to find a location from where I could get a better perspective. Now when I look back, I feel a glimpse of my experiments, my learning progress. I do not consider myself a professional photographer at all. I have still got a lot to learn and still feel that most of my photographs and based on my first few moments of impression. Nevertheless I do want to share some of my amateur experiments.
