The Touch of Homo sapiens

John Samuel

NOTE: Article in Progress

When it comes to the topic of preservation of cultural and human heritage, the first things that come to our mind are physical artifacts namely buildings, paintings, sculptures, clothes, tools etc. Some people are increasingly interested to know the food habits of our ancestors and even the music. Undoubtedly, a lot of efforts are being made both in the national and international levels to protect and preserve the traces of our valuable heritage. But now the touch of Homo sapiens is not confined to these physical artefacts. During the past several decades, human beings have moved several aspects of their lives to computers. Information technology solutions have become integral part of human lives. However the rapid advancement of these solutions by different players has resulted in a situation where some of the older solutions do not work any more on newer machines. Hence there is a growing concern of losing these valuable human endeavours to our future generations.
